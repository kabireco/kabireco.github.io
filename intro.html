Introduction

The Lorenz Curve (named after Max Lorenz) is a graphical representation of the distribution of wealth of an economy. The Lorenz Curve, along with the Gini Coefficient gives a comprehensive view of the income equality of a country.

In the Lorenz Curve shown above (the blue line), we are representing the situation of an ideal economy, wherein the wealth is equally distributed amongst all citizens. The line has a slope of 1 and for every 1% increase in the population considered, there is a 1% increase in the cumulative income. This means that the income and population are in proportion to each other and the curve moves in a straight line.
