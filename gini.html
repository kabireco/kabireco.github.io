Gini Coefficient

The Lorenz Curve represents the ideal situation. In order to apply this to real-life situations, the Gini Coefficient was developed (named after Corrado Gini). The coefficient simply measures how close a particular economy is to the ideal (where there is perfect equitable distribution of income, as discussed earlier).

In the above figure, the line of equality represents the ideal situation and the line underneath represents the curve for a hypothetical economy. We will find how close this curve is to the line of inequality by calculating its Gini Coefficient.

Calculation:

G (Gini Coefficient) = A / (A+B)

A is the area of the difference between the line of equality and our curve. B is the area under our Lorenz Curve. In other words, we are trying to find the ratio of the area of difference A to the total area A + B.
